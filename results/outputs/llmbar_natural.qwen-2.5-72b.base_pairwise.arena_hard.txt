**My Answer:** Vaccinations work by introducing a form of a virus or bacteria, or components of it, into the body. This can be a weakened or inactivated form of the pathogen, or just parts of its structure such as proteins. The immune system recognizes these as foreign and mounts a response, producing antibodies to fight the perceived threat. The immune system also creates memory cells that remember how to quickly produce the right antibodies if the person is exposed to the actual pathogen in the future. This prepares the body to respond more effectively if the actual pathogen is encountered, often preventing illness or reducing its severity.
